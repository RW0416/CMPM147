Experiment 03: Alternate Worlds

Description

This project showcases two procedurally generated “micro worlds” drawn with an 8 × 8-pixel tileset.

Ice World:A frozen plain of snow, ice floes and winding rivers. Occasional pine-like trees punctuate the vast whiteness.
"." - Snow
":" - Ice
"w" - Water
"t" - Tree

Dungeon: A tiny dungeon composed of randomly-sized rooms linked by corridors. Hidden treasure chests and doors provide points of interest.
"_" Background
"." - Rooms
"#" - Corridor
"+" - Door
"C" - Treasure chest

Life: Time-based animation – Every frame, the Ice World re-samples Perlin noise to tint each water tile, producing a “wind-blown” effect.

Technical

Ice World builds a 2-D array of characters by sampling Perlin noise twice and occasionally turning snow into trees; Dungeon repeatedly stamps rectangular rooms into a grid, links them with corridors, then sprinkles doors and treasure chests. Each frame drawGrid() autotiles the array: a 4-bit code describing neighbouring ground feeds a 16-entry lookup that decides which sprite to use.

Reflection

The initial generation is easy, but when I started to implement the autotiles, I became confused. I searched up and found out it is actually a smart system that can connect the tiles smoothly. After I understood the logic, I knew how to implement it, and successfully completed the tasks.
