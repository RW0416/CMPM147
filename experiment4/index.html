Experiment 4: Infinite World

World:

Description

This project contains 3 distinct infinite world.

The first world is consist of islands and ocean. You may plant trees and bushes on the islands.
The second is a space world with stars. You may create a blackhole to eliminate the stars in certain radius.
The third one is a casual games. The world will generate a line of blocks(4 black and 1 white), and your goal is to click on a white block and reverse the color.

You may change the world with different keys, and variations can be restored by entering a previous key.

The world is infinite and have fun!

Technical

This project a infinite world generator based on a p5.js rendering engine that supports "hot-swapping" of multiple procedural worlds. engine.js provides an isometric tile renderer, camera inertia, and a simple callback contract (p3_* hooks). Sketch.js wraps three independent worlds—Island world, a Galaxy, and dynamic Black-and-White Stripes—into one module array; a dropdown menu calls setWorld() to inject the chosen world's hooks at runtime without reloading the page. Randomness is reproducible via XXH32-seeded Perlin noise, visible-tile culling keeps rendering fast, and each world is self-contained, so new worlds can be added by implementing the same hook interface and pushing them into the array.

Reflection
